Ilahi Bahurmat Shaikh al-Mashaikh Rahnuma-e-Kamilin Abul Hasanat Hazrat Khwaja Nisar Ahmad Khaliquei Naqshbandi Damat Barakatuhum al-Aaliyah

BIRTH AND EARLY LIFE
Hazrat Khwaja Nisar Ahmad Khaliquei Naqshbandi — known to his mureeds with boundless love as Sufi Nisar Ahmad or Sain Ji — was born on 11 September 1948 in Faisalabad, a city that would one day witness the flowering of his spiritual grace. From his earliest years he was drawn toward righteousness, humility, and the remembrance of Allah, qualities that later became the hallmarks of his blessed personality.

EDUCATION
Alongside his innate inclination toward the spiritual path, he pursued worldly knowledge with diligence and completed his B.Sc. in Agriculture from the University of Agriculture, Faisalabad. This balance of secular learning and inner enlightenment is itself a reflection of the Naqshbandi ideal: to live in the world yet remain ever turned toward the Divine.

SPIRITUAL ALLEGIANCE (BAY'AT)
The turning point of his life came on 27 December 1967, when he entered the sacred circle of discipleship at the blessed hand of his Murshid-e-Kamil, Hazrat Khwaja Muhammad Sharif Khalique Naqshbandi (may Allah sanctify his secret) — the luminous heir of the Mujaddidi Naqshbandi silsila and the thirty-eighth link in this noble Shajra Mubarak. Through sincere devotion, khidmat, and steadfastness upon the Shari'ah and Tariqah, he traversed the stations of the path under that sacred training.

HEIR OF THE GOLDEN CHAIN
Today he stands as the fortieth jewel in the blessed necklace of the Naqshbandi Mujaddidi order — succeeding Hazrat Pir Sain Muhammad Arif Naqshbandi Khaliquei and carrying forward a trust that stretches from the Master of Creation, Sayyidina Muhammad ﷺ, through the righteous Khulafa and the great Khwajagan of Central Asia, to the revivers of this land. To sit in his company is to feel the continuity of a living silsila, not merely to read of saints in books.

THE VOICE OF IRSHAD
Those who cannot meet him in person have long known his heart through his Irshadat — spiritual discourses that are at once gentle and penetrating. His words do not merely instruct; they awaken. In his teachings, the love of Allah and His Messenger ﷺ is the foundation of true faith; the rights of creation are honoured as a fruit of that love; and the journey toward the Divine passes through the stations of fana — annihilation in the Murshid, in the Prophet ﷺ, and ultimately in Allah Most High.

“The journey to the Divine Essence passes through the stages of annihilation. Until a seeker passes through annihilation in the Guide to reach annihilation in the Prophet ﷺ, the inner self cannot become pure enough to attain the reality of annihilation in Allah.”

He teaches that the sword against the lower self must be drawn with every breath, and that when every moment is spent in Divine pleasure, that is the greatest jihad. He warns against harshness in religion: to label a Muslim as an infidel is not the work of the people of gnosis; rather, the friends of Allah transform even wrongdoers through sublime character and love. Where Divine love speaks, worldly logic falls silent.

“Allah is so Generous that when He accepts a servant, He eliminates all the influences and control of the ego.”

“The best servant is one who bears true love for Allah and His Messenger ﷺ — for it is through that love that one becomes devoted to fulfilling the rights of others.”

MURSHID AND GUIDE
As Abul Hasanat — the father of virtues — and Rahnuma-e-Kamilin (guide of the perfected ones), Hazrat Sufi Nisar Ahmad continues to train seekers upon the Naqshbandi principles of silent dhikr, rabita with the Murshid, suhbat, and strict adherence to the Sunnah. His gatherings are fountains of tranquillity; his gaze heals; his speech softens hardened hearts. He is a living proof that the tariqah of the Khwajagan is not a relic of the past but a light that still burns brightly in our age.

PRAYER
May Allah Most High preserve him in health and honour, lengthen his shadow of blessing over his mureeds, and grant us the ability to walk faithfully in his footsteps upon the path of love, obedience, and inner purification. Ameen.
